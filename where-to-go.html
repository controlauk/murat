Where to go

The land of the flowers

Sudushan Falls

Located about 9 km from Yalova, Sudushan Waterfalls are surrounded by lush green forests and offer a peaceful escape into nature. The sound of cascading water, combined with the fresh forest air, makes it a favorite spot for nature lovers and photographers. Its natural beauty leaves a lasting impression on visitors, making it one of Yalova’s most popular attractions.

Termal Village

Situated around 12 km from the city center, Termal Village is famous for its ancient Turkish baths and natural hot springs, with a history dating back to the Roman era. Known for their therapeutic properties, these baths are believed to help with heart conditions and other ailments. The iconic Sultan’s Bath is a highlight, and the village also offers resorts, hotels, and a serene environment for rest and rejuvenation.

Cinarcik District

Just 15 km from Yalova, Cinarcik is a charming coastal town that combines beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a vibrant summer atmosphere. Popular with both local and foreign tourists, it offers affordable seaside activities, direct views of the Marmara Sea, and easy access to Princess Island and Istanbul. The area is also known for its clean waters and welcoming community.

Atatürk Mansion (Yürüyen Köşk)

A historic wooden house where Mustafa Kemal Atatürk once stayed. The mansion is famous for its unique story of being moved to preserve a nearby tree, becoming a national symbol of environmental awareness. Visitors can explore its preserved rooms and learn about an important chapter in Turkey’s history.

Dipsiz Lake

A hidden natural gem in the mountains near Yalova, Dipsiz Lake is known for its crystal-clear waters and untouched surroundings. This tranquil spot is perfect for hiking, photography, and quiet reflection.
